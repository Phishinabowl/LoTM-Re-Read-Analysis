## Chronological Development

### Novel

#### Chapter 28: Institutional Offer

- What the reader learns: Dunn offers Klein a chance to become a Beyonder and names `Mystery Pryer`, `Corpse Collector`, and `Seer` as available incomplete Sequence 9 options.
- What changes: Klein moves from civilian staff who knows about Beyonders to a candidate for Church-supported advancement.
- What remains unknown: Klein's choice, exact event timing, long-term pathway consequences, and formal-team status remain unresolved.
- Why it matters: The event begins through institutional access and obligation, not private experimentation.

#### Chapter 29: Ability and Risk Framing

- What the reader learns: Dunn explains Seer as a learning-dependent pathway associated with divination and mysticism expertise but weak direct combat ability.
- What changes: Klein and the reader can compare Seer against other options with a clearer idea of tradeoffs.
- What remains unknown: Whether Klein will choose Seer and how the choice will affect him remain unresolved.
- Why it matters: The event's stakes are cognitive and strategic before they become physical.

#### Chapter 30: Klein Chooses Seer

- What the reader learns: Klein tells Dunn that his answer is `Seer`.
- What changes: The event moves from possible advancement to confirmed pathway choice.
- What remains unknown: Whether the potion will succeed, what immediate effects it will have, and how the Nighthawks will classify Klein afterward remain unknown.
- Why it matters: The choice point is distinct from the potion-consumption moment and should remain separately filterable.

#### Chapter 31: Potion Consumption and Status Boundary

- What the reader learns: Dunn warns Klein that the potion is irreversible, takes him to the Nighthawks team's alchemy room, has Old Neil retrieve the formula and materials from Chanis Gate, and confirms Klein will remain civilian staff rather than become a formal team member.
- What changes: Klein consumes the Seer potion and becomes a Beyonder.
- Location detail: The event occurs within the cathedral-linked Nighthawks complex, specifically the alchemy room on the route toward Saint Selena Cathedral, with formula/material access through Chanis Gate.
- What remains unknown: The exact full consequences of the potion, the full training path, and later Sequence access remain unknown.
- Why it matters: This is the event's central status change: Klein becomes a Seer, but not a formal Nighthawk.

#### Chapters 32-33: Immediate Training Aftermath

- What the reader learns: Old Neil confirms Klein's advancement, teaches Cogitation and Spirit Vision, and explains early mysticism concepts; Dunn reinforces caution against dangerous perception.
- What changes: The event produces immediate training obligations and practical safety needs.
- What remains unknown: How far Klein's Seer aptitude differs from normal and how much the Nighthawks understand about his unusual circumstances remain unknown.
- Why it matters: The event is not complete as a story function until the reader sees that advancement requires supervision and control.

#### Chapter 34: First Independent Application

- What the reader learns: Klein applies Old Neil's aura-color explanations while observing Tarot Club participants above the gray fog.
- What changes: The event's consequences leave the Nighthawks setting and begin shaping Klein's independent supernatural activity.
- What remains unknown: Whether the gray fog alters ordinary Seer limitations remains unknown.
- Why it matters: The event has immediate effects on Klein's behavior outside the institution that enabled it.
