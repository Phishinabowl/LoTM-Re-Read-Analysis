## Purpose

Track the reader's developing model of what a Beyonder is: how Beyonders gain power, how Sequences and potion names organize that power, what institutional access looks like, what dangers are known, and what early training/control means.

This page is the broad concept parent for Beyonder status and practice. It should not absorb narrower later concepts such as [Beyonder Characteristics](concept-beyonder-characteristics.md), individual pathways, Sealed Artifacts, or specific mysticism techniques once those topics need their own pages.

## Chronological Development

### Novel

#### Chapter 6: Broad Category and Church Access

- What the reader learns: Beyonders are a known supernatural category, potions are the route to becoming one, Sequences structure the available powers, and orthodox Churches can offer controlled access at the cost of commitment.
- What changes: The setting's supernatural system becomes more procedural than vague folklore.
- What remains unknown: The reader does not yet know how potions are prepared locally, how losing control works, or which specific organizations Klein will encounter.
- Why it matters: This is the first framework for Beyonders as a system rather than isolated miracles.

#### Chapter 18: Formal Nighthawks Explanation

- What the reader learns: Dunn explains Beyonders from an official Church/Nighthawks perspective, including potion formulas, potion ingredients, danger from mismatched or excessive advancement, and the risk of losing control.
- What changes: The abstract Tarot Club explanation becomes institutional doctrine with safety rules.
- What remains unknown: The reader still does not know the local Church's available pathways, Klein's future choice, or the practical experience of becoming a Beyonder.
- Why it matters: Beyonder status becomes dangerous, regulated, and tied to organizational procedure.

#### Chapters 19-22: Beyonders Inside Institutions

- What the reader learns: Nighthawks are official Beyonders serving the Church of Evernight, but their local organization also includes civilian staff. The reader also learns named pathway/Sequence examples such as `Sleepless`, `Midnight Poet`, `Nightmare`, `Mystery Pryer`, and `Spirit Medium`.
- What changes: Beyonder identity separates from job title: a person can be an official Beyonder, civilian staff, pathway holder, or some combination of these.
- What remains unknown: The reader does not yet know whether Klein will become a Beyonder or which pathway he would choose.
- Why it matters: This block builds the institutional and social vocabulary needed to understand Klein's later status boundary.

#### Chapter 21: Roselle's Potion Clue

- What the reader learns: Roselle's diary suggests potion names matter and that "digesting" or "acting" may be more important than simply grasping a potion's power.
- What changes: Potion consumption gains a possible interpretive method beyond official caution, but the clue is not yet fully proven inside Klein's immediate experience.
- What remains unknown: The reader does not know how reliable Roselle's note is, how to apply it, or whether official Beyonder organizations know the same method.
- Why it matters: This is an early confidence-building clue that Beyonder advancement may depend on behavior and self-concept, not only ingredients.

#### Chapters 28-31: Klein Becomes a Church-Supported Beyonder

- What the reader learns: Dunn offers Klein a chance to become a Beyonder through the Nighthawks, names locally available incomplete Sequence 9 options, and clarifies that Klein will remain civilian staff rather than become a formal Nighthawk after consuming the Seer potion.
- What changes: Beyonder status becomes something the protagonist personally acquires, while institutional membership remains a separate category.
- What remains unknown: The reader does not yet know the full consequences of Klein's Seer choice or how unusual his advancement experience may be.
- Why it matters: This is the concept's first protagonist-centered status transition and the clearest early proof that "Beyonder" is not the same thing as "formal team member."

#### Chapters 32-34: Practice, Control, and First Application

- What the reader learns: Old Neil teaches Klein Cogitation and Spirit Vision after the potion succeeds, frames control as necessary for stability, and Dunn warns against probing dangerous perceptions. Alger separately explains Cogitation as daily practice for controlling spirituality and understanding potion powers.
- What changes: Becoming a Beyonder is no longer a single event; it becomes an ongoing practice requiring training, restraint, and interpretation.
- What remains unknown: The reader does not yet know the full relation among Seer traits, Klein's special circumstances, the gray fog, and broader Beyonder rules.
- Why it matters: Early Beyonder power is shown as disciplined perception under risk, not just a list of supernatural abilities.
