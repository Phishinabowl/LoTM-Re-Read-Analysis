## Chronological Development

### Novel

#### Chapter 207: Transfer and Indestructibility

- What the reader learns: Beyonder characteristics are not destroyed or reduced and pass from one carrier to another, including after a Beyonder dies.
- What remains unknown: The practical limits and consequences of dividing or recombining characteristics.
- Why it matters: Characteristics become persistent transferable substances rather than temporary power.

#### Chapter 210: Explicit Divisibility

- What the reader learns: Ince splits a descendant-of-Death characteristic, uses one half for Megose, and retains the remaining half for his own advancement ritual.
- What remains unknown: The broader rules and limits governing characteristic division.
- Why it matters: This is the first verified Volume 1 demonstration that one characteristic can be partitioned between separate uses.

### Donghua

#### Episode 10: Conceptual Precursor

- What the viewer learns: Dead rampaging Beyonders can leave power-bearing items that may become Sealed Artifacts.
- What remains unknown: The formal characteristic term and conservation law.

#### Episode 12: Formal Framework

- What the viewer learns: Beyonder characteristics cannot be destroyed or reduced and pass between carriers.
- What remains unknown: Divisibility is not explained directly.

#### Episode 13: Divisibility Inference

- What the viewer learns: Ince uses the remaining Death-descendant characteristic after an earlier remnant was used for Megose.
- Reader knowledge state: Characteristic divisibility is a strong inference rather than an explicit mechanical explanation.
