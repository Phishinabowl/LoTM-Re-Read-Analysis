## Chronological Development

### Novel

#### Chapter 16: Public Sign at No. 36 Zouteland Street

- What the reader learns: Dunn directs Klein to No. 36 Zouteland Street, where Klein sees the Blackthorn Security Company sign.
- What changes: The Nighthawks' hidden organization gains a mundane public address and cover identity.
- What remains unknown: The reader does not yet know the exact institutional relationship among Blackthorn, the Church, police authority, Saint Selena Cathedral, and Chanis Gate.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn begins as a front door: ordinary enough to exist in public, suspicious enough to invite explanation.

#### Chapter 17: Cover, Contract, and Connected Complex

- What the reader learns: Klein enters Blackthorn, meets Rozanne, signs a confidential civilian-staff contract, and learns that Blackthorn is a disguise. The concealed complex connects with Saint Selena Cathedral and Chanis Gate, and Dunn shows the team's police identity as the Seventh Unit of the Awwa County Police Special Operations Department.
- What changes: Blackthorn becomes the public cover for a Church-linked, police-authorized Beyonder organization.
- What remains unknown: The complete layout, full roster, full hidden facilities, and daily work patterns remain unclear.
- Why it matters: This is the first complete institutional explanation of the location: public commercial cover, hidden Church machinery, police identity, civilian staff, and territory-based jurisdiction.

#### Chapters 20-22: Workplace Logistics and Resource Point

- What the reader learns: Blackthorn functions like a workplace with advance pay, finance handling, equipment issue, ammunition, holsters, police-facing badges, and Nighthawk-linked shooting practice. Klein receives a Seventh Unit badge rather than a Nighthawks badge.
- What changes: The location becomes a practical resource point rather than only a cover story.
- What remains unknown: The reader still does not know Klein's eventual pathway or the full use of the underground complex.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn makes the Nighthawks feel operationally real: pay, paperwork, weapons, credentials, and training all pass through this public-facing office system.

#### Chapters 25-26: Avoided Direct Approach and Cathedral Route

- What the reader learns: While being followed, Klein avoids going directly to Zouteland Street or Blackthorn because that could expose the Nighthawks. He instead uses Saint Selena Cathedral and a concealed route connected with Chanis Gate and Blackthorn.
- What changes: Blackthorn's public cover becomes a vulnerability as well as a convenience; covert access through the cathedral is safer under surveillance.
- What remains unknown: The exact spatial layout and full public/restricted boundary remain unclear.
- Why it matters: The location's cover logic becomes operational: when watched, Klein must not simply walk through the front door.

#### Chapters 30-33: Pathway Choice and Training Route

- What the reader learns: Klein returns to Blackthorn after moving and tells Dunn he chooses `Seer`. Dunn then leads him underground toward the hidden facilities where the alchemy room, Chanis Gate access, and post-potion training context become relevant.
- What changes: Blackthorn becomes Klein's institutional path into becoming a Church-supported Beyonder while still remaining civilian staff.
- What remains unknown: The full facility map and standardized training curriculum remain outside the current boundary.
- Why it matters: The location now supports a complete early advancement pipeline: workplace, captain's office, hidden route, alchemy space, and training environment.

#### Chapters 37-40: Routine Workplace and External Monitoring

- What the reader learns: Klein continues treating Blackthorn as his work/resource point, including ammunition retrieval and planning around Nighthawk-supported practice. Old Neil's restricted-duty work around armory, storeroom, and archives reinforces the hidden-workplace model, while Divination Club monitoring shows work can extend outside Blackthorn.
- What changes: Blackthorn is no longer just where Klein was recruited; it is his ongoing base for Nighthawk-related routine.
- What remains unknown: Full roster, staff schedules, and exact restricted-duty rotation remain incomplete.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn anchors Klein's civilian-staff/Beyonder hybrid status as a day-to-day job.

#### Chapters 41-42: Concrete Layout and Thin Staffing

- What the reader learns: Klein's narration gives the clearest layout so far: reception hall, inner region, Mrs. Orianna's account room, rest room, underground stair, Dunn's office, civilian staff office, and formal Nighthawks' recreation room. Rozanne handles public client intake while formal Nighthawks are spread across cathedral business, Chanis Gate guarding, police assistance, cemetery patrol, leave, and unavailable status.
- What changes: Blackthorn becomes spatially concrete and operationally thin-staffed.
- What remains unknown: Complete map, all rooms, full daily procedures, and full roster remain incomplete.
- Why it matters: The location now behaves like a living workplace, not only a secret base.

#### Chapters 43-47: Notebook Lead and Field-Response Mobilization

- What the reader learns: Klein reports his Antigonus notebook lead at Blackthorn. Dunn mobilizes a team, has Kenley cover Old Neil's armory watch, brings Leonard, Old Neil, Frye, and Klein, uses a Nighthawks carriage, and coordinates with police at the scene. Chapter 47 also reinforces the dual Church/police salary model through Old Neil.
- What changes: Blackthorn becomes the launch point for formal field response.
- What remains unknown: The longer-term handling of the Ray Bieber/notebook lead remains unresolved at this boundary.
- Why it matters: The reader sees how a civilian-staff Seer lead becomes a Nighthawk operation through Blackthorn's command/workplace structure.
