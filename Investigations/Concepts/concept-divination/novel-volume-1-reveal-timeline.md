## Evidence

### Check 1: Familiar Occult Baseline

#### Chapters 4-5

- Chapter 4 foregrounds divination through Klein's preparation for a luck-enhancement ritual and Zhou Mingrui's inherited occult interests.
- Chapter 5 uses the circus fortune-teller and tarot cards to present divination as a reader-familiar practice based on participation and interpretation.
- Why it matters: The concept begins with a mundane/folk baseline before the setting confirms that supernatural mechanics exist behind the practice.

### Check 2: Spiritual Theory

#### Chapter 7

- Alger explains that divination stems from a person's spirituality, spiritual-world connection, and access to information about oneself at a higher level.
- He frames dreams and tarot as tools or signs that require interpretation.
- Why it matters: The reader receives the first supernatural theory for why divination can work.

### Check 3: Seer Method Set

#### Chapter 29

- Dunn explains that the Seer potion is associated with methods including astromancy, cartomancy, spiritual pendulums, and scrying.
- He stresses that the potion grants qualifications and learning ability, not instant mastery.
- Why it matters: Divination becomes a pathway-specific skill set and a reason Klein is attracted to Seer.

### Check 4: Technical Basis

#### Chapter 32

- Old Neil explains that the spirit world does not obey physical-world laws and that past, present, and future may be stacked there.
- He says this is the source of divination, that spirit-world imagery is symbolic, and that divination and many magic spells are cast through Astral Projection.
- Why it matters: The early spiritual theory becomes a practical Beyonder framework with interpretive limits.

### Check 5: Divination Club and Social Practice

#### Chapters 37-38

- Klein notices the Divination Club and connects it to acting as a Seer.
- The club is presented as a place where members provide paid divination, including figures such as Hanass Vincent and Glacis.
- Why it matters: Divination becomes a social/professional practice space, not only a private method.

### Check 6: Spirit Pendulum and Nighthawk Monitoring

#### Chapters 40-41

- Old Neil explains pendulum dowsing/spirit dowsing as a divination method that uses Astral Projection's connection with the spirit world and cosmos, aided by materials such as crystals, gems, or special metals.
- He teaches practical interpretation of pendulum movement and other details of spirit pendulum divination.
- Old Neil also explains that Klein can ask Dunn to approve Divination Club expenses because occult-adjacent clubs may contain cultists or evil-organization members, and the Nighthawks lack enough manpower to monitor them continuously.
- Dunn approves the expense and says Klein can draw the spirit pendulum in the afternoon.
- Why it matters: Divination gains both a concrete method and an institutional monitoring frame.

### Check 7: Field Use and Medium Requirement

#### Chapter 43

- Leonard recognizes that divination requires a medium when the target is absent.
- Klein requests recent clothing or accessories for Elliott, uses Elliott's clothes with his cane, and performs dowsing-rod seeking to determine direction.
- Klein notes that he cannot locate the Antigonus notebook because he lacks even the slightest impression of it.
- Why it matters: This is the first clear field-use proof that divination can solve operational problems while still needing a connection to the target.

### Check 8: Strange Interference

#### Chapters 46-47

- Klein repeats cane divination during the Ray Bieber/notebook search and concludes a strange power is interfering with his divination.
- Chapter 47 reinforces his pressure to act as a Seer by going to the Divination Club.
- Why it matters: Divination is powerful but not absolute; failure and interference become reader-safe mystery signals.

## Interim Conclusions

Through Chapter 47, the reader-safe model is:

1. Divination begins as familiar folk/tarot occult practice.
2. Divination can work because spirituality connects to the spiritual world and symbolic information.
3. Seer gives the qualification to learn divination methods but not automatic mastery.
4. Old Neil's framework ties divination to Astral Projection, spirit-world symbolism, and interpretation.
5. Divination can be practiced socially/professionally through clubs.
6. Nighthawks may monitor occult-adjacent divination spaces.
7. Practical field divination can require mediums and target impressions.
8. Divination can be blocked or distorted by strange supernatural interference.

Do not import later gray-fog divination rules, later method refinements, or the final explanation of specific interference sources into this concept page until the relevant reader boundary advances.
