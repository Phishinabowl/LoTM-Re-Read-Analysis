## Evidence

### Check 1: Early Ritual-Like Occult Practice

#### Chapter 4

- What happened: Klein prepares a luck-enhancement ritual using inherited occult knowledge before the setting's supernatural rules have been explained.
- Why it matters: This gives prayers/rituals a familiar occult baseline before the reader can distinguish folk practice, real mysticism, and later supernatural access systems.

### Check 2: Practical Ritualistic-Magic Context

#### Chapter 33

- What happened: Old Neil's post-potion instruction expands into ritualistic magic context while teaching Klein practical mysticism basics.
- Why it matters: Rituals become part of supervised Nighthawk/Beyonder practice rather than only pre-system occult atmosphere.

### Check 3: Field-Sealing Effect

#### Chapter 46

- What happened: Old Neil uses Spirit Vision and identifies a ritual-like sealing effect during the Ray Bieber / Antigonus notebook field response.
- Why it matters: The reader sees that ritual effects can leave field evidence and alter a scene even when the full procedure and practitioner remain unknown.

## Interim Conclusions

Through Chapter 47, the reader-safe model is:

1. Ritual-like actions first appear as familiar occult practice.
2. Nighthawk instruction confirms ritualistic magic as part of practical mysticism.
3. Ritual effects can be interpreted in the field by someone with relevant expertise.
4. Character-specific prayer addresses, delivery rituals, bestowal channels, and later Tarot Club prayer systems are not yet reader-safe at this boundary.

Do not import later prayer wording, gray-fog mechanics, messenger rituals, or high-sequence ritual systems until the relevant reader boundary advances.
