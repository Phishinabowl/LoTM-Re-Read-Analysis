## Question

How does the novel build the reader's understanding of Beyonders through Chapter 34, and which pieces of that understanding should be preserved as filterable knowledge units?

## Evidence

### Check 1: Broad Category and Access Model

#### Chapter 6

- The Tarot Club material introduces Beyonders as people who gain supernatural power through potions.
- The same framework links Beyonder access to Sequences and to orthodox Church-controlled routes.
- Why it matters: The reader receives the first system-level model before the Nighthawks are fully revealed.

### Check 2: Official Risk Doctrine

#### Chapter 18

- Dunn provides the official Church/Nighthawks explanation of Beyonders, including potion formulas, ingredients, advancement danger, and the risk of losing control.
- Why it matters: Beyonder power becomes regulated and dangerous rather than merely mysterious.

### Check 3: Institutional and Social Status

#### Chapters 19-22

- The Nighthawks are revealed as official Beyonders under the Church of Evernight.
- The team also includes civilian staff, establishing that the organization is not made only of formal Beyonders.
- Named Sequence examples appear through Church discussion, including `Sleepless`, `Midnight Poet`, `Nightmare`, `Mystery Pryer`, and `Spirit Medium`.
- Why it matters: Beyonder identity, pathway title, job role, and organization membership begin to separate into distinct categories.

### Check 4: Potion Interpretation Clue

#### Chapter 21

- Roselle's diary suggests the importance of potion names, digestion, and acting.
- Why it matters: This is an early clue that Beyonder practice may involve interpreting and embodying the potion name, but it is not yet a full reader-safe doctrine.

### Check 5: Protagonist Status Transition

#### Chapters 28-31

- Dunn offers Klein a chance to become a Beyonder through Church/Nighthawks support.
- Dunn explains locally available incomplete Sequence 9 options and frames the practical tradeoffs.
- Klein chooses `Seer`.
- Klein consumes the Seer potion in the Nighthawks' alchemy room, with formula/material support tied to Chanis Gate.
- Dunn explicitly states that Klein is becoming a Beyonder but will remain civilian staff rather than a formal Nighthawk.
- Why it matters: The concept becomes protagonist-centered, and the novel clearly separates supernatural status from formal team membership.

### Check 6: Immediate Training and Control

#### Chapters 32-33

- Old Neil confirms Klein's successful advancement and begins teaching Cogitation and Spirit Vision.
- Old Neil and Dunn frame Beyonder practice as something requiring restraint, control, and safety awareness.
- Why it matters: Becoming a Beyonder has immediate training consequences and cannot be treated as a single static status change.

#### Chapter 34

- Alger separately describes Cogitation as daily practice for controlling spirituality and understanding potion powers.
- Klein also applies Old Neil's aura-color framework above the gray fog.
- Why it matters: The Nighthawks' practical instruction is corroborated by another Beyonder source, and Klein immediately uses new perception outside the Nighthawks setting.

## Interim Conclusions

Through Chapter 34, the reader-safe model is:

1. Beyonders are humans who gain supernatural power through potions.
2. Beyonder power is organized by Sequences and named pathways/potions.
3. Beyonder power is dangerous and can involve losing control.
4. Orthodox Churches and official teams regulate much visible access.
5. Beyonder status is separate from formal team membership.
6. New Beyonders require training, control, and careful interpretation of perception.

Do not import the formal Beyonder-characteristics law, later pathway structure, demigod mechanics, later acting-method explanations, or later advancement rules into this concept page until the reader boundary reaches them.
