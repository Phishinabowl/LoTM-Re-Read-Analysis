## Evidence

### Check 1: Death-Scene Notebook Before Full Identification

#### Chapter 1

- Klein wakes to an open notebook on the desk beside books, ink, and a pen.
- The notebook contains the striking written line associated with the original Klein's death.
- The reader does not yet know whether this is the same ancient notebook later discussed by Welch/Naya/Dunn or a separate ordinary notebook.
- Why it matters: The novel begins with a notebook as part of the death-scene mystery, but the reader cannot yet safely identify the full artifact chain.

### Check 2: Antigonus Family Notebook Named Through Klein's Records

#### Chapter 9

- Klein reviews the original Klein's records about helping Welch and Naya decipher an old notebook.
- The first deciphered page identifies the author as a member of the Antigonus family.
- The notes connect the notebook to the Fourth Epoch, Solomon Empire, the Black Emperor, Tudor, and the "Nation of the Evernight" at the Hornacis mountain range.
- Klein infers that the missing June 27 entry and the death-scene line may be connected to the notebook.
- Why it matters: The reader receives the notebook's first meaningful identity: it is an old Antigonus family text with dangerous historical content.

### Check 3: Missing Object and Nighthawk Lead

#### Chapter 13

- Dunn tells Klein that Welch, Naya, and Klein are connected to the whereabouts of the Antigonus family notebook.
- Dunn says no trace of the Fourth Epoch notebook was found at the suicide scene, making Klein the only remaining lead.
- Why it matters: The notebook becomes the missing object at the center of the Nighthawks' early investigation.

#### Chapters 24-26

- Klein frames the object to his history teachers as a Solomon Empire / Antigonus family diary obtained by Welch.
- Azik reacts to the name Antigonus as if it should mean something to him, though he cannot remember.
- Dunn says the Nighthawks are investigating where Welch received the notebook and cannot rule out other people or organizations seeking it.
- Why it matters: The notebook starts connecting the academic-history layer, Azik's memory problem, and broader supernatural pursuit.

### Check 4: Secret Order Connection and Pathway-Access Motive

#### Chapters 28 and 31

- Dunn identifies the intruder's background as the Secret Order.
- Dunn offers Klein a Beyonder chance partly because Klein may help find the Antigonus notebook and because danger remains around him.
- Dunn later reports that the Secret Order may be temporarily unable to confirm whether the notebook is important to them.
- Why it matters: The notebook is tied to an ancient organization and becomes one of the reasons Klein receives controlled Church/Nighthawks pathway access.

### Check 5: Hornacis/Flegrea Danger Signal

#### Chapters 32 and 46

- After Klein becomes a Seer, he hears the repeating Hornacis/Flegrea murmurs.
- In Chapter 46, Klein connects Hornacis back to decoded notebook content.
- Klein treats the similarity as potentially dangerous and avoids thinking deeper to reduce losing-control risk.
- Why it matters: The notebook's decoded content is not only historical information. It appears connected to a dangerous supernatural signal that can affect Klein directly.

### Check 6: Tarot Club Historical Stake

#### Chapter 35

- Klein shares that the Secret Order has lost an Antigonus family notebook.
- Alger says the news is known beyond the Tingen Nighthawks and the Secret Order, then wonders how the Church of Evernight would react.
- Alger states that the Antigonus family was destroyed by the Church of Evernight, though he is uncertain whether this happened near the end of the Fourth Epoch or early in the present epoch.
- Klein realizes the Nighthawks' interest in the notebook is more serious than he had appreciated.
- Why it matters: The notebook's significance becomes historical and institutional, not merely investigative.

### Check 7: Klein's Lead and Nighthawk Field Response

#### Chapters 45-47

- Klein's intuition points to the apartment across from the kidnappers as connected to the Antigonus notebook.
- Klein reports to Blackthorn, and Dunn mobilizes a field team including Leonard, Old Neil, Frye, and Klein.
- The notebook is not found, but the apartment contains dangerous traces and reinforces the Hornacis/Flegrea connection.
- Old Neil says the Secret Order will likely infer the Ray Bieber warrant is notebook-related and redirect its focus toward finding Ray and the notebook before the Nighthawks.
- Why it matters: The notebook becomes the object around which Klein's Seer sensitivity, Nighthawk procedure, and the Nighthawks/Secret Order race converge.

## Interim Conclusions

### Reader-State Structure

Through Chapter 47, the notebook develops in layers:

1. Mysterious death-scene object.
2. Fourth Epoch / Antigonus family historical text.
3. Missing object from Welch and Naya's deaths.
4. Nighthawks investigative priority.
5. Possible Secret Order target.
6. Source of Hornacis/Flegrea danger signals.
7. Historical stake involving the Church of Evernight.
8. Operational target in the Nighthawks/Secret Order race around Ray Bieber.

### Artifact Boundary

At Chapter 47, the reader knows the notebook is dangerous, missing, historically important, and sought by multiple parties. The reader does not yet know its complete contents, current location, how it produces or relates to supernatural danger, why Ray Bieber is tied to it, or what larger causal role it may play.
