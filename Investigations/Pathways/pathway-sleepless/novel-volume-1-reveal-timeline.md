## Evidence

### Check 1: First Word-Level Mention

#### Chapter 15

- Dunn uses `Sleepless` casually during the recruitment conversation, joking that even they are not immune to dry historical documents making people sleepy.
- Why it matters: The term appears before it is explained, so Chapter 15 should be recorded as first word-level mention but not first meaningful pathway disclosure.

### Check 2: First Practical Character Example

#### Chapter 17

- Rozanne says Leonard had trouble on the stairs on his first day after becoming a `Sleepless`.
- This ties Sleepless to a person, a new power/status change, and the Tingen Nighthawks workplace before the reader receives the formal Sequence ladder.
- Why it matters: Leonard is the first practical example, but the reader still does not know his exact place in the ladder.

### Check 3: Leonard as Midnight Poet

#### Chapter 21

- Leonard introduces himself as `Sequence 8's Midnight Poet`.
- Klein remembers that the Church's complete pathway begins with Sequence 9 and vaguely recalls Rozanne mentioning Sleepless.
- Why it matters: The reader gets Leonard's Sequence 8 title one chapter before Rozanne formally links it to the Sleepless pathway.

### Check 4: Formal Ladder and Ability Profile

#### Chapter 22

- Rozanne identifies the Church's complete starting Sequence as `Sequence 9 Sleepless`.
- She explains basic Sleepless traits: reduced sleep requirement, seeing through darkness without lights, and increased physical strength, intuition, and mental capability deeper into the night.
- She places `Midnight Poet` at Sequence 8 and `Nightmare` at Sequence 7.
- Klein immediately connects Nightmare to Dunn's earlier dream guidance and asks about the captain; Rozanne's reaction supports the inference.
- Rozanne says she does not know what comes above Sequence 7 and suggests perhaps only Captain would know.
- Why it matters: Chapter 22 is the pathway's first full reveal block: sequence ladder, ability profile, Church ownership, Leonard context, and Dunn inference.

### Check 5: Complete Church Route Versus Incomplete Options

#### Chapter 28

- Dunn says Klein could give up the immediate chance and wait for room to become a `Sleepless`.
- Dunn calls Sleepless the first complete Sequence the Goddess bestowed on the Nighthawks.
- He then names the available incomplete Sequence 9 options: `Mystery Pryer`, `Corpse Collector`, and `Seer`.
- Why it matters: The reader learns Sleepless is not merely known by the Church; it is the complete Nighthawks route, but access depends on institutional capacity.

### Check 6: Sleepless as Contrast for Exhaustion

#### Chapter 33

- After Spirit Vision practice exhausts Klein, Old Neil notes that they are not Sleepless and therefore need sleep after excessive practice.
- Why it matters: Sleepless remains a practical benchmark for endurance even after Klein chooses Seer.

### Check 7: Team Distribution Snapshot

#### Chapter 42

- The Nighthawks deployment snapshot identifies Royale Reideen and Kenley White as `Sleepless`.
- The same snapshot identifies Seeka Tron as a `Midnight Poet`.
- Leonard is guarding Chanis Gate, Dunn is at the cathedral, Frye is a Corpse Collector, and Old Neil/Klein are not formal field-ready members in the same way.
- Why it matters: Sleepless becomes the visible pathway backbone of the Tingen team rather than only a Leonard/Dunn clue.

### Check 8: Leonard's Ability in the Elliott Case

#### Chapter 44

- Leonard uses singing that makes Klein feel torpid and serene, and later explains that the targets will rationalize the effect as ordinary sleepiness or a dream.
- Why it matters: This is the first practical ability expression tied to Leonard's Midnight Poet status, showing the pathway in operation without overdefining the full power set.

### Check 9: Dunn as Advanced Sleepless

#### Chapter 45

- Leonard refers to Dunn as an advanced Sleepless who only needs two hours of sleep during the day.
- Why it matters: This confirms Dunn's pathway affiliation more directly than the Chapter 22 inference, while the exact `Nightmare` Sequence remains best recorded as strong evidence rather than a direct profile-confirmed fact.

## Interim Conclusions

### Reader-Safe Sleepless Model Through Chapter 47

By Chapter 47, the reader can safely know:

1. `Sleepless` first appears as unexplained Nighthawk vocabulary in Chapter 15.
2. Leonard is first tied to Sleepless in Chapter 17 and names himself `Sequence 8 Midnight Poet` in Chapter 21.
3. Chapter 22 explains the known Church ladder: `Sleepless -> Midnight Poet -> Nightmare`.
4. Sequence 9 Sleepless is associated with reduced sleep, night/darkness advantages, physical strength, intuition, and mental capability.
5. The Church/Nighthawks hold Sleepless as their complete pathway route, but access depends on available room.
6. Dunn is confirmed on the Sleepless pathway by Chapter 45 and strongly implied to be Nightmare by Chapter 22.
7. Royale Reideen, Kenley White, and Seeka Tron add additional Tingen-team pathway-status examples in Chapter 42.
