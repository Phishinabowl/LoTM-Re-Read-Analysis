## Evidence

### Check 1: First Word-Level Mention

#### Chapter 21

- Roselle's diary says he regretted not choosing `Apprentice`, `Seer`, or `Marauder`.
- The same diary passage also gives Klein the acting/digestion clue for potions.
- Why it matters: The reader sees `Seer` as a potentially important pathway name before knowing whether Klein can access it.

### Check 2: Church-Held Pathway Option

#### Chapter 28

- Dunn says that apart from the complete `Sleepless` pathway, the Church has three Sequence 9 potion formulas: `Mystery Pryer`, `Corpse Collector`, and `Seer`.
- Dunn says the local Church only has Sequence 9 of `Seer`, and he is unsure whether the Holy Cathedral has later Sequences.
- Klein immediately connects the option to Roselle's diary regret.
- Why it matters: `Seer` becomes a concrete institutional option for Klein, but its continuation is uncertain from the Tingen reader position.

### Check 3: Ability Profile Before Selection

#### Chapter 29

- Dunn says the proper question is what consuming the Seer potion gives rather than what "Seers" have in the abstract.
- He names associated methods including astromancy, cartomancy, spiritual pendulums, and scrying.
- He clarifies that consuming the potion does not immediately grant mastery; it gives qualifications and capacity to learn.
- Dunn says Seers lack direct combat methods but are more learned and professional in mysticism than Mystery Pryers.
- Why it matters: The reader gets an early functional profile: Seer is knowledge/divination-oriented, learning-dependent, and weak in direct combat.

### Check 4: Klein Chooses Seer

#### Chapter 30

- Klein tells Dunn that his answer is `Seer`.
- Why it matters: The pathway moves from option to protagonist commitment.

#### Chapter 31

- Klein compares Seer against Mystery Pryer and treats Roselle's regret as enough to tip the balance.
- Old Neil prepares the Seer potion in the Nighthawks' alchemy room with formula/material access through Chanis Gate.
- The formula includes ingredients such as pure water, night vanilla, gold mint leaves, poison hemlock juice, dragon blood grass, Lavos Squid blood, and Star Crystal.
- Old Neil notes Star Crystal is excellent material for creating divination crystals.
- Why it matters: The Seer pathway is now embodied in a concrete formula, Church/Nighthawks access process, and Klein's actual advancement.

### Check 5: First Effects and Training

#### Chapter 32

- Klein confirms that he is now considered a Seer.
- Old Neil points out a physical manifestation in Klein's eyes and says Cogitation will help converge the power.
- Old Neil teaches Cogitation and Spirit Vision, while also explaining Soul Body, Astral Projection, spirituality, and the spirit-world/source relationship behind divination.
- Why it matters: The reader sees the pathway's early powers as perception and interpretive training, not instant fortune-telling mastery.

#### Chapter 33

- Klein tests Spirit Vision, learns how to switch it off, and receives Dunn's warning not to probe dangerous murmurings or existences.
- Dunn says Old Neil reported that Klein was a suitable candidate because he could use Spirit Vision even without Cogitation; Klein guesses this may be a Seer trait.
- Why it matters: Seer aptitude is associated with perception, but the story emphasizes danger and control alongside capability.

#### Chapter 34

- Klein applies Old Neil's aura-color explanations while observing Tarot Club participants above the gray fog.
- Alger separately explains Cogitation as daily practice for controlling spirituality and grasping potion powers.
- Why it matters: Seer-related training begins influencing Klein's independent supernatural observation immediately, while broader potion practice is corroborated outside the Nighthawks.

## Interim Conclusions

### Reader-Safe Seer Model Through Chapter 34

By Chapter 34, the reader can safely know:

1. `Seer` is one of the pathway names Roselle regretted not choosing.
2. The Church of Evernight's Tingen team can offer only Sequence 9 `Seer` locally.
3. Later Seer Sequences are unknown from Dunn's local perspective.
4. Seer is associated with divination, mysticism expertise, Spirit Vision, and interpretive perception.
5. Seer lacks strong direct combat methods at the current boundary.
6. Klein chooses and consumes the Seer potion through Church/Nighthawks support.
7. Klein's first Seer training is supervised by Old Neil.
