## Evidence

### Check 1: First Evernight References as Religion and Public Institution

#### Chapter 1

- Klein's mother is identified as a devotee of the Evernight Goddess.
- Why it matters: This is the novel's first Evernight reference, but it presents the faith as part of Klein's family background rather than introducing the Church as a Beyonder institution.

#### Chapter 3

- The Church of Evernight is associated with Sunday schools and the earlier provision of inexpensive education.
- The narration places it alongside the other orthodox religions whose competition shaped the kingdom's religiously neutral public-school system.
- Why it matters: This is the first meaningful presentation of the Church as a public institution embedded in ordinary Loen society.

### Check 2: First Nighthawks Name and Broad Institutional Context

#### Chapter 6

- Alger tells Audrey that becoming a Beyonder through an orthodox Church requires joining it and accepting its conditions.
- Audrey replies that she already knows about the Nighthawks, Mandated Punishers, and Machinery Hivemind but does not want to lose her freedom.
- The chapter does not individually map each named organization to its parent Church.
- Why it matters: The reader learns the name `Nighthawks` and understands it as one of the official organizations connected broadly with orthodox Churches before meeting the Tingen team.

### Check 3: First Tingen Nighthawks Appearance Under Police Cover

#### Chapter 10

- Dunn Smith and Leonard Mitchell first appear as members of a police party questioning Klein about Welch and Naya.
- Dunn and Leonard are not yet named to Klein in the scene and do not identify themselves as Nighthawks.
- Their unusual interest in the occult incident and Leonard's parting remark suggest that they know more than ordinary police, but their true affiliation remains hidden.
- Why it matters: This is the first appearance of known Tingen Nighthawks, but not the point where the reader can identify them as such.

### Check 4: Dunn's Investigation and Nighthawk Disclosure

#### Chapter 12

- Dunn returns to Klein's home, now giving his name but continuing to operate as a police inspector.
- He uses a guided dream to investigate Klein's missing memories and possible involvement in the deaths.
- Klein wakes from the dream and finds Dunn waiting outside his room.
- Why it matters: The apparently ordinary investigation is revealed to involve Beyonder power, but Dunn's exact affiliation is still withheld until the next chapter.

#### Chapter 13

- Dunn reintroduces himself as a Nighthawk.
- Klein immediately connects the title to the Church of Evernight's Beyonder teams mentioned during the Chapter 6 gathering.
- Dunn explains that he entered and guided Klein's dream rather than creating it outright.
- Why it matters: The novel joins two previously separate knowledge tracks. The reader can now identify the supposed police investigators as Church of Evernight Beyonders and understand the Nighthawks as an operational team rather than only a name.

### Check 5: Recruitment Becomes Possible

#### Chapter 14

- After the investigation at Welch's residence, Dunn suggests that Klein could join as civilian staff so the Nighthawks can monitor any lingering danger.
- Dunn also raises the possibility that Klein might eventually become a Beyonder.
- He explains that becoming a Nighthawk costs freedom and that Klein cannot freely select a pathway because he is neither clergy nor a devotee.
- Why it matters: The Nighthawks change from outside investigators into a possible institutional home for Klein, while the reader learns that Church affiliation governs access to Beyonder power.

#### Chapter 15

- Dunn clarifies that civilian staff are still part of the organization and receive access to relevant confidential knowledge.
- He explains that most team members and civilian staff come from within the Church and that exceptional recruitment is constrained by secrecy.
- Dunn formally invites Klein to join as civilian staff.
- He states that civilian salaries and benefits are funded by both the Church and the police department.
- Why it matters: The reader learns that the Nighthawks combine Church authority, police cooperation, secrecy, and a mixed staff of Beyonders and civilians.

### Check 6: Blackthorn Cover, Membership, and Jurisdiction

#### Chapter 16

- Klein follows Dunn's directions to No. 36 Zouteland Street and sees the Blackthorn Security Company sign.
- Why it matters: The Nighthawks' public-facing cover is revealed before its exact legal and institutional structure is explained.

#### Chapter 17

- Klein enters Blackthorn, meets Rozanne, and learns that the concealed complex connects with Saint Selena Cathedral and Chanis Gate.
- Klein accepts Dunn's offer and signs a five-year confidential contract, formally becoming civilian staff.
- The contract is enforceable through the Church of Evernight's tribunal courts.
- Dunn confirms that Blackthorn Security Company is a disguise and shows the team's police identity as the Seventh Unit of the Awwa County Police Special Operations Department.
- Dunn explains that the unit handles supernatural incidents involving Evernight followers within its assigned Tingen territory. He contrasts its jurisdiction with the Mandated Punishers under the Church of Storms and the Machinery Hivemind in Tingen.
- Dunn states that the Tingen team has six formal Nighthawks and six civilian staff after Klein joins.
- Why it matters: This is the first complete operational model of the Tingen Nighthawks: a Church-affiliated Beyonder team with police authority, territorial jurisdiction, a civilian component, and a mundane commercial cover.

### Check 7: Ethos, Chanis Gate, and Sealed Artifact Custody

#### Chapter 18

- Dunn explains the dangers of losing control and the Church's cautious advancement rules.
- He quotes Archbishop Chanis's description of the Nighthawks as guardians who continually struggle against danger and madness.
- Why it matters: The reader receives the moral and psychological foundation of Nighthawk service, not merely its organizational structure.

#### Chapter 19

- Dunn introduces the team's civilian duties and explains that Church and police departments supply the organization.
- He identifies Chanis Gate as part of the modern Nighthawks system and says major cities have one beneath their central cathedral.
- Formal Nighthawks guard the gate alongside Church-sent Keepers. The secured areas contain formulas, materials, prisoners, classified records, and Sealed Artifacts.
- Dunn explains the Church's four-grade Sealed Artifact classification and the clearance required for access and use.
- Why it matters: The Church is established as a custodian of dangerous supernatural knowledge, people, materials, and artifacts, with formal security and clearance systems extending beyond the local team.

### Check 8: Workplace Logistics, Equipment, and Early Pathway Hints

#### Chapter 20

- Dunn sends Klein to Mrs. Orianna for advance pay, establishing ordinary workplace finance beneath the supernatural cover.
- Dunn authorizes Klein to collect demon hunting bullets and normal bullets, keep Welch's revolver, and practice at the shooting range.
- Dunn says most of the underground shooting range at No. 3 Zouteland Street belongs to the police department, but one range is specifically for the Nighthawks.
- Why it matters: The Nighthawks become a working institution rather than just exposition: they have finance, paperwork, ammunition, badges, training spaces, and police-linked facilities.

#### Chapter 21

- Old Neil gives Klein demon hunting bullets, normal bullets, a holster, and a Seventh Unit, Special Operations Department badge.
- Klein notes that the badge is not a Nighthawks badge, preserving his current civilian-staff boundary.
- Old Neil says the Holy Cathedral permits local copies of Roselle scripts for research, showing that information custody can include sanctioned research duplicates rather than only locked originals.
- Leonard introduces himself as Sequence 8 `Midnight Poet`.
- Why it matters: Chapter 21 deepens the dual identity model: Klein receives police-facing credentials and Nighthawk resources while still lacking formal Nighthawk status. It also starts attaching pathway identities to individual team members.

#### Chapter 22

- Klein asks Rozanne about the starting point of the Church's complete Sequence.
- Rozanne says the Church's complete pathway begins with Sequence 9 `Sleepless`.
- She describes `Sleepless`, names Sequence 8 `Midnight Poet`, and identifies Sequence 7 as `Nightmare`.
- Klein connects `Nightmare` to Dunn entering his dream; Rozanne implies Dunn is one of Tingen Church's two Sequence 7 Beyonders.
- Rozanne also names Old Neil's Sequence 9 as `Mystery Pryer` and says she knows an incomplete Sequence 7, `Spirit Medium`, associated with Daly.
- Why it matters: This is the first practical pathway map for the Church of Evernight. The reader learns the local Nighthawks are not all on the same pathway, but the Church's complete native line is `Sleepless -> Midnight Poet -> Nightmare`.

### Check 9: Saint Selena Cathedral as Operational Access Point

#### Chapter 24

- Melissa's friend Selena is noted as being named after St. Selena by Evernight-believer parents.
- Why it matters: This is a small public-religion detail rather than a Nighthawks operational reveal. It may belong later in a Saint Selena or public-faith thread, but it does not materially change the Church/Nighthawks model by itself.

#### Chapter 25

- While being followed, Klein avoids going directly to Zouteland Street because that could expose the Nighthawks.
- He instead enters Saint Selena Cathedral, which is publicly ordinary for an Evernight believer.
- Klein presents his Seventh Unit, Special Operations Department badge to a bishop and asks for Dunn Smith.
- Why it matters: The cathedral becomes a practical safe contact point for the hidden Nighthawks structure rather than just the public headquarters named in Chapter 17.

#### Chapter 26

- A priest guides Klein through a concealed route connected with Chanis Gate and Blackthorn Security Company.
- Klein infers that the follower avoided the cathedral because they may fear the Church or a Beyonder bishop.
- Dunn treats the tailing as a Nighthawk operational problem and sends Klein back out through the cathedral route to draw out the observer.
- Why it matters: The Church's visible sacred space, hidden Beyonder infrastructure, and police-facing Nighthawk function all operate together in a live incident.

## Interim Conclusions

### Opening Disclosure Structure

The novel does not introduce the Church of Evernight and the Nighthawks in one exposition block. It moves through distinct reader states:

1. Evernight as Klein's inherited family religion.
2. The Church as an ordinary public institution.
3. `Nighthawks` as the unexplained name of an official Beyonder organization.
4. Dunn and Leonard as apparently ordinary police investigators.
5. Dunn's use of supernatural power.
6. Dunn's explicit identity as a Nighthawk of the Church of Evernight.
7. Recruitment, Blackthorn, police authority, jurisdiction, staffing, and Church oversight.
8. The Nighthawks' ethos and the Church's custody of supernatural dangers.
9. Workplace logistics, equipment, badges, shooting practice, and first pathway map.
10. Saint Selena Cathedral as a public-safe and hidden-operational route.

This staging means first mention, first appearance, first identifiable appearance, and first complete institutional explanation must remain separate disclosure points.

### Dunn and Leonard's First Appearance

The user's memory was directionally correct. Dunn and Leonard first approach Klein under police cover in Chapter 10. However, the reader has already encountered the name `Nighthawks` in Chapter 6 and cannot identify these two investigators as members until Dunn's Chapter 13 disclosure.

### Blackthorn Security Company

Blackthorn is first seen as a sign at the end of Chapter 16. Chapter 17 confirms it as a disguise and explains the Tingen team's simultaneous Church, police, and public-facing identities.

### Bridge Arc: Workplace and Operational Access

Chapters 20-26 do not introduce a new parent organization, but they materially change how real the Church/Nighthawks structure feels to the reader. The team now has pay, equipment, ammunition, badges, shooting practice, archives, individual pathway identities, and a practical cathedral route for covert contact.

### Pathway Boundary Before Chapter 28

By Chapter 22, the reader knows the Church of Evernight's complete pathway begins with `Sleepless` and reaches `Midnight Poet` and `Nightmare` by Sequence 7. The reader has also heard `Mystery Pryer` and `Spirit Medium` as incomplete pathway examples, but Klein has not yet made his own pathway choice.
