## Evidence

### Check 1: First Practical Mention and Pathway Introduction

#### Chapters 17, 21, and 22

- Chapter 17 first mentions Leonard through Rozanne's workplace anecdote about his first day after becoming a Sleepless and failing to control his new powers on the stairs.
- Chapter 21 introduces Leonard directly as a black-haired, green-eyed, poet-like Nighthawk who names himself `Sequence 8 Midnight Poet`.
- Leonard tells Klein that surviving the Welch/Naya incident without immediate Nighthawk protection makes Klein special, then privately wonders if something noticed anything.
- Chapter 22 connects Midnight Poet to the Church of Evernight's complete Sleepless pathway ladder.
- Why it matters: Leonard enters as a practical example of Sleepless-pathway transformation before becoming a visible teammate, and his first direct scene immediately plants the "special people/hidden perception" frame around Klein and himself.

### Check 2: Suspicion, Deduction, and First Partner Mission

#### Chapters 38-47

- Chapters 38-39 show Leonard questioning the official explanation for why Klein survived while Welch and Naya died, proposing alternative notebook/vessel possibilities, and privately retaining doubts after Klein leaves.
- Chapter 42 identifies Leonard as guarding Chanis Gate when most formal Nighthawks are deployed elsewhere, then has him accept the Elliott Vickroy kidnapping commission when Blackthorn lacks available security personnel.
- Chapters 43-44 pair Leonard with Klein for the Elliott case. Leonard accepts the mission, recognizes that missing clothes can serve as a divination medium, supports Klein's dowsing-rod seeking, uses singing to make the kidnappers sleep or rationalize the effect, breaks the door lock, subdues the kidnappers, and praises Klein's shooting.
- Chapters 45-46 bring Leonard into the Ray Bieber/Antigonus notebook field response. He drops his frivolity when Dunn mobilizes the team, drives the Nighthawks carriage, breaks into the sealed apartment, fetches patrolling police, and helps process the Ray Bieber warrant.
- Why it matters: This arc moves Leonard from suspicious coworker to operational partner. The article should not compress it into "Leonard helps with Elliott" because it establishes his deduction, charm, pathway ability, combat style, police-cover function, and first shared secret pressure with Klein.

### Check 3: Sealed Artifact 2-049 and Leonard's Hidden Difference

#### Chapters 71-80

- Chapter 71 places Leonard in the team responding to Sealed Artifact 2-049 and the Ray Bieber lead; Dunn assigns him carriage duty and he notifies front-office staff.
- Chapter 72 has Leonard drive according to 2-049's direction, advise Klein to divine danger around them, pull Klein away from a fireball, and guard 2-049 with Borgia after the artifact becomes the largest battlefield danger.
- Chapters 73-74 show Leonard participating in the fight against Ray Bieber, including shooting and singing with Aiur Harson.
- Chapter 76 reveals that Leonard recovered earlier than Klein expected and watched Klein act around 2-049 and the suited clown. Leonard says he wanted to help but saw Klein did not need it, then compares Klein's specialness to his own.
- Chapter 77-80 keep Leonard on aftermath duty, including guarding the site, supporting police follow-up, and contributing to the investigation that traces the escaped clown's temporary residence.
- Why it matters: This is the first major evidence that Leonard is hiding something beyond ordinary Midnight Poet competence. The reader can suspect a secret but cannot yet identify it.

### Check 4: Church Procedure, Team Meetings, and Chanis Gate Protocol

#### Chapters 88-89, 96-97, 105-109

- Chapter 88 has Leonard on night duty when Klein reports the Selena/Elizabeth supernatural incident; Dunn later tells Klein that he and Leonard checked Hanass Vincent before Hanass died in his sleep.
- Chapter 89 confirms that Leonard and Dunn wore police uniforms when Klein first met them, tying Leonard's first appearance to official police-cover procedure.
- Chapter 97 has Leonard guide Klein to Gawain, explain Gawain's knightly background, jokingly discuss poetry and the acting method, and handle the combat-training arrangement under police contract.
- Chapter 105 shows Leonard arriving with Dunn and Kenley after Klein's Sirius encounter, joking that Klein keeps meeting Beyonders and evil forces while still listening to the report.
- Chapter 106 places Leonard in the Thursday Nighthawks meeting.
- Chapters 108-109 show Leonard as the first responder to Klein's Chanis Gate alarm. He participates in protocol suspicion, uses prayer/recital to confirm Klein is still safe, explains prior Chanis Gate loss-of-control risks, helps reason about the Misfortune Cloth Puppet anomaly, and proposes the Antigonus notebook connection.
- Why it matters: Leonard is not just dramatic. He knows records, protocols, police cover, history, combat-training logistics, and the working danger model around Chanis Gate.

### Check 5: Abnormal Deaths, Life-Force Hypothesis, and Trissy

#### Chapters 117-127

- Chapter 117 keeps Dr. Daxter secret from Leonard despite trust, showing that Nighthawk protocol can compartmentalize even trusted teammates.
- Chapter 118 has Dunn assign Klein to work with Leonard and Frye on a spike in ordinary-looking deaths. Leonard briefs Klein on the death-rate anomaly, team split, and need for divination/ritual support.
- Chapters 119-120 show Leonard leading field questioning in the Lower Street/workhouse cases, displaying both social awareness and class-conscious commentary while helping interpret Klein's dream-divination evidence.
- Chapter 121 gives Leonard's major hypothesis: the victims are people already near death whose life force has been shortened or stolen for summoning/curses. Leonard explicitly tells Klein that he knows Klein is special after 2-049 and hints that he himself is different from ordinary Beyonders.
- Chapters 122-123 turn the hypothesis into the Trissy/Demoness operation. Leonard's idea leads to Klein's confirmation, a house discovery, and the Nighthawks pincer. During the fight, Leonard participates in the dream trap, shooting, poetry/recital, altar pressure, and curse countermeasure after Trissy retrieves his blood-stained flower.
- Chapters 124-126 continue the aftermath: Leonard's tune signals safety, he helps identify Trissy as transformed from Tris, accepts Klein's gray-fog-supported train warning under the cover of a reliable tip, and acts quickly enough to trigger a broad search even though Trissy escapes.
- Chapter 127 clarifies that Slumber Charms overlap with Midnight Poet/Nightmare abilities, so Leonard does not need them.
- Why it matters: This is Leonard's densest middle-Volume arc. It establishes his investigative originality, hidden knowledge, trust tension with Klein, combat vulnerability, and practical willingness to use Klein's irregular divination without forcing direct disclosure.

### Check 6: Acting Method, Old Neil Aftermath, and Morse Town

#### Chapters 143, 154-162

- Chapter 143 mentions that Dunn and Leonard taught Klein revolver maintenance, a small but concrete mentorship/workplace detail.
- Chapter 154 shows Leonard recognize the term "digesting" when Klein says he is applying for Sequence 8, worry that Klein's advancement is risky, accept the broader coincidence concern, mutter that he may need to hurry up, and immediately pull Klein into sharing his experience with the team.
- Chapter 155 shows the emotional shock that Klein's acting-method explanation has on Old Neil; Leonard's earlier push helps the team hear the lesson before Old Neil's crisis.
- Chapter 160 has Leonard and Klein discuss Leonard's secret directly before a mission; Leonard warns Klein not to dream of discovering it, then challenges Klein to try.
- Chapters 160-162 show Leonard leading or co-leading the Morse Town haunting response with Kenley and Klein: he manages priest/townsfolk expectations with deliberately performative holy items, assigns Klein ritual display work, identifies weak ghost traces through Midnight Poet sensitivity, interprets Southern Continent inverted-pyramid/Death symbolism, gathers local stranger reports, and recommends safer Sealed Artifact watch rotation.
- Chapter 162 also shows Leonard notice abnormal sunlight from Klein's hidden Flaring Sun Charm ritual, then later examine Sealed Artifact 3-0782 seriously and with confusion.
- Why it matters: Leonard's secret, sensitivity, practical experience, and protective suspicion continue alongside comedy. The article should preserve both registers.

### Check 7: Madam Sharon Surveillance Training and Bridge Work

#### Chapters 194-195

- Chapter 194 has Dunn assign Klein to learn tailing and monitoring from Leonard and Frye for the Madam Sharon commission.
- Leonard's guidance advises against reckless direct tailing before understanding the target's routine and recommends using Hound Pub/mob channels to build a schedule.
- Why it matters: This is a bridge arc, but not filler. Leonard becomes part of Klein's practical policing/tradecraft education.

### Check 8: Lanevus Clue and Final Crisis Lead-In

#### Chapters 203-205

- Chapter 203 has Dunn inform Klein through dream that Leonard found a clue in the Lanevus case and needs Klein's help at 62 Howes Street.
- Leonard explains that after ordinary investigation failed, he shifted to cross-checking residents connected to Sirius Arapis and identified a suspicious rental tied by witness recognition to Lanevus.
- Leonard asks Klein to divine hidden compartments or secret chambers, leading to the discovery of Lanevus's warning letter.
- Chapters 204-205 place Leonard in the office response to Lanevus's warning and the Megose arrival.
- Why it matters: Leonard supplies the immediate operational clue that exposes Lanevus's hidden residence and leads directly into the Tingen endgame.

### Check 9: Megose Battle and Leonard's Survival

#### Chapters 205-210

- Leonard is present when the team shifts from Lanevus's warning to the Megose crisis.
- The surviving aftermath establishes that Leonard lived through the battle in which Dunn and Klein died and Saint Selena's ashes were lost.
- Chapter 210's retrospective story reframes the whole Tingen chain through 0-08, but Leonard's personal understanding is not complete until Chapter 211's telegram.
- Why it matters: Leonard is both participant and survivor, but the reader should not over-attribute his understanding before the Holy Cathedral warning is read.

### Check 10: Holy Cathedral Warning, Red Gloves, and Family Notification

#### Chapter 211

- After the disaster, Leonard is numb, isolated, and puzzled that the murderer took Klein's Beyonder characteristic but left Dunn's behind.
- Seeka brings an unencrypted Holy Cathedral telegram from Dunn's office, sent before or during the battle but unread in time, warning about Ince Zangwill and Sealed Artifact 0-08.
- Leonard connects the warning to Ince and 0-08, regains focus, and declares his intention to apply to the Red Gloves.
- Seeka notes the Red Gloves' Sequence 7 threshold and high danger; Leonard says he is close to advancing and internally focuses on revenge against Ince.
- Leonard then chooses to notify the families, explicitly taking on the scene he least wants to face.
- Leonard tells Melissa and Benson that Klein died as a hero, discusses compensation, and later helps carry Klein's coffin.
- Why it matters: This is Leonard's Volume 1 turning point. He becomes a revenge-driven survivor whose path now points beyond Tingen into the Church's mobile elite pursuit structure.

## Interim Conclusions

Through Volume 1, Leonard's reader-safe shape is:

1. A black-haired, green-eyed, poet-like Tingen Nighthawk.
2. A confirmed Sequence 8 Midnight Poet in the Church of Evernight's Sleepless pathway.
3. An experienced formal teammate who alternates between frivolity and seriousness.
4. One of Klein's earliest suspicious readers inside the story.
5. A field partner in the Elliott, Ray Bieber/2-049, Trissy, Morse Town, Madam Sharon, Lanevus, and Megose arcs.
6. A source of deduction and operational hints that repeatedly push investigations forward.
7. A character with an explicitly acknowledged but unidentified secret.
8. A survivor of the Tingen disaster who learns Ince/0-08's connection and chooses the Red Gloves path for revenge.

Do not identify the source or nature of Leonard's hidden secret beyond Volume 1 evidence. The reader knows Leonard is unusual and not an average Beyonder; the reader does not yet have later-volume explanation.
