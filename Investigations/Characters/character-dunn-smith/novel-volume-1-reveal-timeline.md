## Evidence

### Check 1: Police Cover, First Name, and Nighthawk Reveal

#### Chapters 10, 12, and 13

- Chapter 10 first presents Dunn visually/functionally as the unnamed gray-eyed police inspector handling Klein during the Welch/Naya investigation.
- Chapter 12 gives the first explicit name, Dunn Smith, when the same gray-eyed inspector arrives at Klein's door, confronts him with carriage-driver evidence and Welch's missing revolver, and then tests him through a dream/nightmare escape sequence.
- Chapter 13 reintroduces him explicitly as Nighthawk Dunn Smith.
- Chapter 13 also clarifies that Dunn entered and guided Klein's dream, confirms Klein's memory loss, identifies the missing Antigonus family notebook as central, and frames Klein as the only surviving lead.
- Why it matters: Dunn's reveal is staged in layers. The reader first sees mundane police authority, then a named investigator with impossible dream access, then an official Church/Nighthawks Beyonder.

### Check 2: Clearance, Recruitment Logic, and Confidentiality

#### Chapters 14-17

- Chapter 14 shows Dunn bringing Klein to Daly at Welch's residence, accepting Daly's spirit-medium examination, and then allowing Klein to return home while warning him not to reveal the incident.
- Chapter 14 casually introduces two possible future paths: civilian staff or becoming a Beyonder.
- Chapter 14-15 explains why the invitation is possible: Klein survived, has useful historical training, and has already been exposed to the secret, so recruitment does not newly violate confidentiality.
- Chapter 15 makes civilian staff part of "one of us" while keeping Beyonder status stricter.
- Chapter 15 explains the secrecy doctrine: public knowledge of the supernatural would create more trouble, not less.
- Chapter 15 gives the practical employment frame: salary, five-year contract, lifetime confidentiality after leaving, shifts, vacation constraints, and a concealed contact method through Hound Pub because the team's location is still confidential before Klein agrees.
- Chapter 17 brings Klein to Blackthorn Security Company, has him sign the confidential contract, and lets him address Dunn as Captain.
- Why it matters: Dunn's recruitment role is not just friendly guidance. It establishes the Church/Nighthawks as an institution with confidentiality law, employment terms, restricted location access, and operational hierarchy.

### Check 3: Blackthorn, Special Operations Cover, Team Structure, and Official Doctrine

#### Chapters 17-18

- Chapter 17 confirms Blackthorn Security Company as a disguise and also reveals a police-department cover identity: Seventh Unit, Special Operations Department, Awwa County Police.
- Chapter 17 gives the Tingen team size at this boundary: six formal Nighthawks including Dunn and six civilian staff including Klein.
- Chapter 17 establishes that Blackthorn can accept ordinary security jobs as long as they do not interfere with daily operations.
- Chapter 18 makes Dunn the reader's first major official explainer for Beyonder doctrine: potion origins, Sequences, improper advancement, fixed pathways, remnant spiritual influence, and losing control.
- Chapter 18 includes the Nighthawks' promotion-risk rule: even meritorious teammates must wait and be examined before promotion.
- Why it matters: Dunn becomes the person who translates hidden supernatural danger into bureaucratic systems, job roles, and safety doctrine.

### Check 4: Civilian Tasks, Chanis Gate, Sealed Artifacts, and Pathway Hints

#### Chapters 19-22

- Dunn explains civilian staff and team structure.
- Dunn assigns Klein to walk the likely routes connected to the missing Antigonus notebook and to read historical/canonical materials in the armory.
- Dunn introduces Chanis Gate, including formal Nighthawk rotation, Keepers, traps, potion formulas, magical materials, prisoners, classified records, and Sealed Artifacts.
- Dunn introduces Sealed Artifact grades and 0-08's basic reader-safe profile without revealing its actual mechanism.
- Chapter 20 characterizes Dunn's forgetfulness while showing his protective administration: advance pay, demon hunting bullets, normal bullet practice, and shooting training authorization.
- Chapter 21 gives Klein his Special Operations badge and introduces Leonard as Sequence 8 Midnight Poet, while Leonard says he has to replace Captain.
- Chapter 22 confirms the Church's complete pathway starts with Sequence 9 Sleepless, proceeds to Sequence 8 Midnight Poet, and then Sequence 7 Nightmare. Klein connects Nightmare to Dunn's dream guidance; Rozanne says it is likely Dunn is one of Tingen's two Sequence 7 Beyonders.
- Why it matters: Dunn appears as captain of a dangerous but regulated organization, while the reader can strongly infer his Sleepless/Nightmare pathway association without the narration needing an explicit formal profile card.

### Check 5: Secret Order Pressure and Klein's Beyonder Offer

#### Chapters 24-28

- Chapters 25-26 show Klein seeking Dunn through Saint Selena Cathedral/Blackthorn after noticing surveillance.
- Chapter 26 has Dunn connect the surveillance either to Welch's family investigation or to the Antigonus notebook.
- Chapter 27 shows Dunn entering Klein's dream again to warn him of an intruder and coordinate the response.
- Chapter 28 identifies the intruder's organization as the Secret Order, links the Antigonus family diary/notebook to them, and frames the remaining Secret Order members as hard to capture.
- Chapter 28 offers Klein a Beyonder chance because of his contribution, continued danger exposure, and the usefulness of heightened perception in finding the notebook.
- Chapter 28 names the local incomplete Sequence 9 choices: Mystery Pryer, Corpse Collector, and Seer, while Sleepless remains the complete Church pathway option that requires waiting for sufficient room.
- Why it matters: Dunn turns danger exposure into controlled institutional advancement.

### Check 6: Seer Choice, Potion Process, and Status Boundary

#### Chapters 29-33

- Dunn explains Seer tradeoffs and divination-related methods.
- Dunn lets Klein wait until Monday rather than forcing an immediate pathway decision.
- Chapter 30 has Klein choose Seer.
- Chapter 31 confirms Dunn's warning that consuming the potion leaves no room for regret.
- Dunn leads Klein toward the team's alchemy room, arranges for Old Neil to retrieve the Seer formula/materials, and states the status boundary: Klein becomes a Beyonder due to danger/notebook needs but remains civilian staff, not a formal Nighthawk.
- Dunn reinforces safety doctrine after Klein's advancement.
- Why it matters: Dunn is the authorizer and boundary-setter for Klein becoming a Beyonder.

### Check 7: Resource Approval and External Monitoring

#### Chapters 37-41

- Chapters 37-39 show Klein continuing Dunn's assigned search around the Antigonus notebook and retrieving/practicing with his daily bullet allocation.
- Chapter 40 has Old Neil tell Klein that joining the Divination Club can be framed as Nighthawk monitoring of an occult-adjacent civilian space and that Dunn can approve the expense.
- Chapter 41 has Dunn sign Klein's Divination Club expense request and fold it into quarterly funding from the Church and police department.
- Why it matters: Dunn's authority includes mundane administration, not just supernatural command.

### Check 8: Office Administration, Thin Staffing, and Field Command

#### Chapters 42-47

- Chapter 42 shows the team thinly spread: Dunn is away at the cathedral, Leonard is guarding Chanis Gate, Frye and Royale assist police, Kenley is on leave, Seeka is on cemetery patrol, Old Neil is frail, and Klein is a novice.
- Chapter 45 has Klein report the Antigonus notebook/Ray Bieber lead at Blackthorn.
- Dunn mobilizes Leonard, Old Neil, Frye, and Klein, orders Kenley to replace Old Neil's armory watch, has Klein repeat the details, and puts Klein/Old Neil in the safer rear position during entry.
- Chapter 46 shows Dunn coordinating scene handling, using Leonard and ordinary police, questioning for Ray Bieber, and issuing the next procedural step: a warrant for Ray Bieber for the murder of his mother.
- Chapter 47 records that ordinary Blackthorn commission money follows Captain's rules, while Dunn himself has not returned by noon.
- Why it matters: Dunn's current boundary closes with active field leadership.

## Interim Conclusions

Through Chapter 47, the reader-safe model is:

1. Dunn first appears as an unnamed official investigator under police cover, is named in Chapter 12, and is explicitly revealed as a Nighthawk in Chapter 13.
2. He is revealed as a Nighthawk tied to the Church of Evernight.
3. He is the Tingen Nighthawks captain and operational authority.
4. He recruits Klein into confidential civilian-staff work.
5. He explains official Beyonder doctrine, Chanis Gate, Sealed Artifact secrecy, and 0-08's reader-safe appearance.
6. He authorizes Klein's controlled path to becoming a Seer.
7. He manages resources, external monitoring, and team deployment.
8. He mobilizes the field response around Klein's Antigonus notebook lead.

Do not import later 0-08/Megose/final crisis material into this character page until Dunn's reader boundary advances beyond Chapter 47.
