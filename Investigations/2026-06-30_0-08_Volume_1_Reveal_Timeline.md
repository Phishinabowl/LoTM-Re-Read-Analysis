# 0-08 Volume 1 Reveal Timeline

## Question

What does the novel reader know about 0-08 at each meaningful point in Volume 1, and which events can be attributed to it retrospectively by the end of the volume?

## Status

In Progress. Checks 1 and 2 are EPUB verified.

## Scope

### Endpoint Event

The complete Volume 1 reader-disclosure timeline, ending with the Chapter 210 story and Klein's interpretation in Chapter 212.

### Investigation Window

Volume 1, from the first mention in Chapter 19 through Chapter 212.

## Evidence

### Check 1: First Reader Mention

#### Chapter 19

- Dunn explains that Grade 0 Sealed Artifacts are extremely dangerous, highly important, and subject to the highest confidentiality.
- During that explanation, Dunn retrieves Ince Zangwill's wanted notice from his own drawer and shows it to Klein.
- The notice explicitly identifies Ince as a former archbishop and failed Gatekeeper who escaped with Sealed Artifact 0-08.
- Dunn says that 0-08 looks like an ordinary quill and can write without ink, but he does not explain its function.
- Old Neil is still alive; after this conversation, Dunn sends Klein to him for reading materials.
- Why it matters: The novel names 0-08 and establishes its danger, appearance, and connection to Ince very early, while withholding what the artifact actually does.

### Check 2: Retrospective Causal Beginning

#### Chapter 210: Story

- The written account begins with Secret Order member Regence mistakenly selling the Antigonus notebook because of weariness and illusions.
- The account characterizes this as a logically plausible coincidence.
- The notebook then influences successive owners, passes through several hands, and reaches Sirius Arapis and Hanass Vincent in Tingen.
- The chapter presents the rest of the Tingen sequence as a developing story whose rows can be crossed out or revised when people disrupt the intended course.
- At the end, Ince Zangwill is shown holding the inkless quill and writing the conclusion of Tingen's story.
- Why it matters: The notebook's arrival is not presented as the first isolated manipulation. It is the outcome of an earlier transfer chain incorporated into 0-08's story.

#### Chapter 212: Avenger

- Klein reconstructs Ince Zangwill's motive and concludes that the series of events was plotted to obtain Saint Selena's ashes and complete Ince's advancement.
- Klein then considers 0-08 the likely power behind the arrangements and the many coincidences.
- He also infers that the artifact must have restrictions because Ince could not simply write an immediate, effortless victory.
- Why it matters: Klein's reasoning converts Chapter 210's narrative demonstration into the reader's practical understanding of 0-08 as the mechanism coordinating the plot.

## Interim Conclusions

### Check 1

The novel first mentions 0-08 explicitly in Chapter 19. The introduction occurs during Dunn's explanation of Sealed Artifact grades, not after Old Neil's death. At this point the reader knows its designation, danger class, appearance, inkless writing, and connection to Ince Zangwill, but nothing about its reality-shaping function.

The user's remembered scene belongs to the Donghua presentation and may represent a later, recontextualized adaptation reveal. Its exact episode and disclosure details remain unverified.

### Check 2

The user's memory was directionally correct: Chapter 210 reveals the Antigonus notebook's arrival in Tingen as part of the opening setup of 0-08's written story.

The earliest disclosed manipulation is more precisely the chain that causes the notebook to move toward Tingen. It begins with Regence mistakenly selling it under the influence of weariness and illusions, then continues through successive ownership changes until it reaches Tingen.

Chapter 210 provides strong contextual evidence that 0-08 orchestrated this chain. Chapter 212 supplies Klein's explicit reader-facing inference that 0-08 arranged the coincidences behind Ince Zangwill's plot.
