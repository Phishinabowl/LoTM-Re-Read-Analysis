## Evidence

### Check 1: Public Sign and Cover Reveal

#### Chapters 16-17

- Chapter 16 shows the Blackthorn Security Company sign at No. 36 Zouteland Street.
- Chapter 17 confirms Blackthorn as a disguise for the Tingen Nighthawks.
- Chapter 17 also establishes Klein's confidential civilian-staff contract, tribunal authority, Seventh Unit police identity, and the connection to Saint Selena Cathedral and Chanis Gate.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn becomes the public front for a Church-linked, police-authorized Beyonder team.

### Check 2: Workplace Logistics and Equipment

#### Chapters 20-22

- Dunn sends Klein for advance pay and authorizes weapon/ammunition support.
- Old Neil issues bullets, a holster, and a Seventh Unit badge.
- Nighthawk-specific shooting practice and police-linked training space appear in this block.
- Rozanne's pathway discussion and Leonard's `Midnight Poet` identity make Blackthorn a place where team roles and pathway identities become ordinary workplace knowledge.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn becomes a working institution with resources, credentials, pay, and internal knowledge boundaries.

### Check 3: Cathedral Route and Cover Vulnerability

#### Chapters 25-26

- Klein avoids going directly to Zouteland Street or Blackthorn while followed because it could expose the Nighthawks.
- He uses Saint Selena Cathedral and a hidden route connected with Blackthorn and Chanis Gate.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn's public cover has operational rules. It can be useful, but it can also become risky under surveillance.

### Check 4: Pathway Choice and Hidden Facilities

#### Chapters 30-33

- Klein returns to Blackthorn and tells Dunn he chooses `Seer`.
- Dunn leads him underground toward the alchemy room and connected restricted facilities.
- Old Neil supervises Klein's early Seer practice in the Nighthawks environment.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn is the visible front door for Klein's transition into Church-supported Beyonder status.

### Check 5: Routine Base and External Monitoring

#### Chapters 37-40

- Klein continues using Blackthorn as a routine work/resource point.
- Old Neil's restricted-space duties around armory, storeroom, and archives reinforce the hidden-workplace model.
- Divination Club monitoring shows that work can extend beyond Blackthorn while still being approved through Nighthawk procedure.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn anchors Klein's ongoing civilian-staff/Beyonder role.

### Check 6: Layout and Staffing

#### Chapters 41-42

- Chapter 41 provides the clearest practical layout: reception hall, inner region, account room, rest room, underground stair, Dunn's office, civilian staff office, and formal Nighthawks' recreation room.
- Chapter 42 lists the team's current deployment and absences, showing why only limited public-client response is possible at that moment.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn becomes a concrete, thinly staffed workplace rather than an abstract secret base.

### Check 7: Notebook Lead and Field Response

#### Chapters 43-47

- Klein's Seer-related search produces the notebook/Ray Bieber lead.
- Klein reports the lead at Blackthorn.
- Dunn mobilizes a field team, arranges coverage for Old Neil's armory watch, brings relevant personnel, and coordinates with ordinary police at the scene.
- Chapter 47 reinforces the dual Church/police funding model through Old Neil's salary.
- Why it matters: Blackthorn is the command/workplace point where a clue becomes formal Nighthawks field action.

## Interim Conclusions

Through Chapter 47, the reader-safe model is:

1. Blackthorn is first seen as a mundane company sign.
2. It is confirmed as the public disguise for the Tingen Nighthawks.
3. It is connected to Saint Selena Cathedral, Chanis Gate, and hidden restricted facilities.
4. It functions as Klein's workplace, pay point, equipment point, and reporting base.
5. It can be unsafe to approach directly under surveillance.
6. It supports pathway selection and access to hidden advancement/training facilities.
7. It has a concrete office layout and staffing pattern by Chapter 42.
8. It functions as the launch point for the Antigonus notebook field response.

Do not import later crisis material involving Megose, 0-08, Ince, or Blackthorn's final Volume 1 role until this location's reader boundary advances.
